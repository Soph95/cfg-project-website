London is one of the biggest tourist attractions in the world. In this guide you will see some of the best attractions, food places and events you can visit all in a day.

Morning: Buckingham Palace

How to arrive

Address: Westminster, London SW1A 1AA. Buckingham palace is located in the city of Westminster, to get directions and information on how to get there click on the map below!

Recommendations

Buckingham Palace is one of the most popular attractions in London. There are many interesting things you can see whilst visiting the palace. You are able to see the queens guards in their iconic red uniform during the changing of the Queen's Guard ceremony. This is a formal ceremony in which the group of soldiers currently protecting Buckingham Palace are replaced by a new group of soldiers and the ceremony is free to watch! The State Rooms are now part of a tour that features many pieces from the Royal Collection, including paintings, sculptures, rare porcelain and fine period furniture. You are also able to see the throne room, the music and picture gallery on the tour.

Food

Whilst visiting Buckingham Palace, there are plenty of great food places you can enjoy around the area, ranging from traditional british cafes to Mediterranean and Italian restaurants. Click here to see a full list of food places you could visit nearby.

Afternoon: The Tower of London

How to arrive

Address: St Katharine's & Wapping, London EC3N 4AB. The Tower of London is located on the north bank of the River Thames in central London. If you would like to see directions on google maps then click on the map below!

Recommendations

There are many things to see within the Tower of London, this includes The Jewel House which has been home to the Crown Jewels since 1968. The collection includes St. Edward's Crown, made of pure gold and still used in the crowning of British sovereigns. You can also go on the Yeoman Warden tour, which is a guided tour led by the guards of the tower famously known as 'beefeaters'. The tour usually lasts around one hour and you can learn about some of the interesting people who were imprisoned, tortured and executed as well as the Tower’s fascinating history.

Food

Whilst you are there, there are plenty of affordable food places you can enjoy around the area, ranging from traditional british, asian and French restaurants. Click here to see the a full list of food places.

Evening: The Shard

How to arrive

Address: 32 London Bridge St, London SE1 9SG. Click on the image of the map below to see directions.

Recommendations

The shard is a 95-storey supertall skyscraper. The View from The Shard is officially the highest viewpoint in the UK and the perfect starting point for those wanting to explore the city as it provides a stunning birds-eye overview of the streets and landmarks below. Many people visit the shard just for the view alone and take it as an opportuinity to take spectacular photos of the capital. There is also a good selection of bars within the shard you could visit that offer live music and display sport events as well as a great view.

Food

The shard offers a great range of restaurants, that would be perfect for enjoying an evening meal, many restaurants are located on higher levels of the buliding which allows visitors to enjoy their meal whilst being sorrounded by the views of London.
